Sechaba Community Care: Empowering Families Through Victim Empowerment Programs

Providing Psychosocial Support Services to Men, Boys, and Their Families.

Sechaba Community Care was officially registered in the Non-Profit Organisation registry with the Department of Social Development in the year 2010. After the process of registration, Sechaba Community Care met its objectives towards helping the community to live an improved quality life by assisting in the restoration of family values, ensuring a safe and healthy environment.

GOAL: Restoring Family Values, Promoting a safe and Healthy Environments

We at Sechaba take a stand to strive as a beacon of hope in community development

Observing the prevalence of gender-based violence in communities, which affects the smooth functioning of families, men often feel unprotected by government policies and programs. Some end up as victims of gender-based violence with no avenue to report or share their challenges. The negative influence of culture and male upbringing in families has further contributed to the current societal status. Sechaba Community Care, having served families in the community, has decided to introduce Victim Empowerment services and Perpetrators intervention.

Taking a Stand Against Gender-Based Violence.

Fathers and Sons Men’s Voices: Empowering Men to Protect and Support Their Families.

This initiative will focus on early intervention and prevention of gender-based violence that affects families, particularly women and children, through the Fathers and Sons Men’s Voices program. This program aims to prepare future men to protect and support their families without resorting to abuse. Additionally, it will focus on prevention strategies such as awareness campaigns for men, women, and children to share their feelings and change behaviors.

Transforming Societal Norms Through Prevention Programs.

Raising Awareness and Promoting Behavioral Change for a Violence-Free Future.

As it will prepare the future men to be able to protect and support their families without any form of abuse. The programme will also focus on the Prevention such as Awareness/Campaign for Men, Women and children to be able to share their feelings and change of behaviour.
